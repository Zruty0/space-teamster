# Estella II, Acheron

Equal parts paradise and perdition, from the idyllic cloud city of Olympos to the bitter penal hell of Pandemonium, Acheron is, per capita, the richest planet in the system.

Its economy and its extreme concentration of wealth is predicated upon the rarity of hydrogen on Acheron; indeed the only significant source of hydrogen comes from the sulfuric acid clouds, and must otherwise be imported. As one might imagine 'waste' water and other hydrocarbons is very, very strictly collected.

In this document we first outline the three primary locations of interest on -- or more properly, sometimes, "above," -- Acheron, then the Cerberus Human Resources corporation, then the key personalities that control what is arguably the most beautiful, but most dangerous, world in all the Estella system.

## Places

There are three key places in Acheron. The cloud-city of Olympos, its "sister" surface penal colony of Pandemonium, and the nominally independent Union station of Nimbus Crucible.

### Olympos

The cloud city of Olympos has no rival as being the most decadent places in the system -- for those that can afford it. It grew from a simple aerostat CO2 refinery operation, positioned high above Acheron's crushing surface atmosphere some many kilometers high, at standard atmospheric pressure. Few sights are considered more beautiful than the sunrises and sunsets as viewed from Olympos.

#### Districts

1. **Carbonvale**: What would eventually be named Olympos started as a simple CO2 refinery operation for the mass production of oxygen for the inner Hearth -- CO2 being comprising most of the atmosphere, and solar power being readily available -- and carbon materials (carbon fiber, graphite, and graphene) system-wide. It was hence named Carbonvale. Quaint though it may seem, the district of the city where this sort of "traditional" industry of the planet takes place still bears that name. CO2 refining and carbon-based-material manufactory remains the primary productive industrial activity in Olympos, and is how many "honest" people continue to earn a living -- as small a minority as such honest people may be at the current moment.

2. **Hub**: Once plans for the planet and this aerostat settlement expanded beyond simply Carbonvale, a sort of "central" hub to connect future "domes" was constructed. This is the center of the city, containing the city council, as well as the infrastructure to move goods and services to all the other auxiliary domes, including docking facilities for arriving atmosphere-capable starships.

2. **Cerberus Headquarters**: The headquarters of the Cerberus Human Resources corporation, and its primary 'exchange' port with the surface settlement of Pandemonium, effected not by starships but by long "dumbwaiter" chains of crystalline graphene to haul and lower great containers of cargo.

3. **Paradiso**: The resort and vacation district, and a retirement place for the very wealthy. Few citizens of even modest means in the Estella system consider their lives complete unless they've gone here at least once. The perfect 'tropical' temperature, dramatic clouds, and beautiful sunrises and sunsets one can find make this world into what is, to visit for a short time, a paradise. Under the great carbon-fiber enhanced silica glass dome of Paradiso, one can either gaze at the sky, or go below to view the lower observation deck into the stormy atmosphere of the planet below.

4. **Weathervane**: Silas Vane's own private district for himself, his family, and his servants. A perfect paradise at least for one man, though he graciously allows general visitors to tour through his district and witness -- though not partake -- in its absurdly lavish delights -- once every Estellan year. While the nominal political center of Olympos lies elsewhere, if you one really wants to see which way the wind is blowing -- check the Weathervane.

5. **Embassy**: The ambassadorial district.

#### Cerberus Human Resources

The most powerful entity on Olympos is the Cerberus Human Resources (CHR) corporation. It is the most efficient means of extracting profit from human misery known to history.

THe business worked in this way. There were and are in the Estella system many autocratic regimes -- and these all had people that they wanted to dispose of. Sometimes it was a handful of politically difficult people that the leader's didn't quite like, other times it was a certain ethnic profile that the government had promised to dispose of.

This was done through execution and extermination programs, Silas Vane here saw waste, and so, opportunity: rather than kill them, load them onto one-way "bulkers" to "colonize" the surface of Acheron. Through this peculiar species of mercy, CHR was born.

The value proposition CHR offered was simple: governments buy "shares" in CHR by delivering people they don't want, to the CHR's custody. The CHR then utilizes those prisoners to perform surface mining and refining on and within Acheron -- effected not through cargo starships, but a chain which delivers material mined by the prisoners to Olympos -- and the profit from the proceeds are delivered to the shareholders.

It is worth noting that the power of CHR extends far beyond Acheron, but as their headquarters and seat of power is here, it is worth noting.

### Pandemonium

Pandemonium is the only significant human "city" on Acheron's surface. It is nominally an "asset" of the Cerberus Human Resources corporation, if not actually under their direct control.

Originally, Cerberus Human Resources's Deep Crust Extraction Site 4 (DCES-4), Pandemonium is arguably the most secure prison that could be conceived. It has no guards, no wardens, no watch towers, no monitoring of any sort. It needs none: the brutal reality of hydrogen scarcity and the environment outside allows the CHR to maintain control.

The graphene-crystal chain that connects Olympos to Pandemonium works according to a brutal logic: Pandemonium sends up refined rare metals -- typical gold, silver, platinum, uranium, aluminum, silica -- and after assay of the resources, a set amount of an "exchange" set of molecular hydrogen is sent back down.

Pandemonium has no ability to produce hydrogen of its own. The only conceivable source comes from the sulfuric-acid rain, but even when rains do come that evaporates long that evaporates long before it hits the surface. No hydrogen, no water. No water, no life.

This may seem unfair. That is the point. In this way, Olympos is able to maintain a profit margin on precious metal extraction that makes even astroid-extraction companies blush, and all simply at the cost of matter that makes up nearly three-quarters of all matter in the universe -- except on Acheron of course.

All transport to and from Pandemonium is done by this chain. Due to the adverse conditions, any starship traversals are nearly impossible -- except for the most skilled pilots. The CHR's control over access to Pandemonium is in this way almost absolute.

Almost.

### Nimbus Crucible

Far deeper in the atmosphere, indeed within the sulfuric acid clouds, one finds the aerostat station of Nimbus Crucible. Being bathed every day in acid, the structural requirements of this station are fairly extreme, but it nonetheless plays an essential role in the economy of Acheron: through its processing of the hydrogen-sulfide clouds, it is the sole economically feasible source of hydrogen native to Acheron.

It is a relatively "small" place. It is barely a kilometer across, mostly automated, and has a crew of only perhaps forty, ten of which are actual workers and the rest a staff of researchers under the direction of the administrator, Dr. Colette Renard, climatologist and planetologist.

Dr. Renard's official mission is simple: see if Acheron can become another Earth-Like world with the correct support -- whether it will take an L1-shade, or whatever, the Union's goals are clear.

Nimbus Crucible is perhaps the simplest place of Acheron -- a research and resource gathering station. That's all it is. Honest.

## People

### Silas Vane

The founder of Cerberus Human Resources. Even at his age and having presided over the economic engine that built Olympos to being the Jewel of Estella, in his elder years he still remains a robust, energetic man, still possessing that cold inhumanity that allowed him to found CHR in the first place.

The one compromise he has made to his age is that he has stepped back from active management of CHR, leaving it, much to the dismay of his grandson Elias, to the management of his senior executives and the board of directors.

He spends his time enjoying the pleasures of his private district of Olympos, whether it be his perfect gardens, the epicurean delights of his expansive kitchen, or his own harem -- yes, not absolutely every woman that is rendered to CHR is sent down to Pandemonium, at least, not immediately.

He is a man now beyond all human law and justice, but though he knows it well, paradoxically, the thought almost seems to weary him. He has not had an honest conversation in twenty years.

### Thomas Thorne

On his home planet and nation, Thomas Thorne was a charismatic but failed revolutionary. He and his 'conspirators' -- so dubbed -- were sentenced to transport. Once they took delivery of him, the CHR assigned him a low 'potential' value, their actuarial staff projecting likely death by overwork in perhaps six months. Instead, he became the prisoner's first effective leader.

The 'Pandemonium' name's Miltonian roots are evident, and it fits: if Silas Vane is "God" who constructed a Hell in which he may condemn souls and so torture them for all eternity, Thorne is the "Satan" who rallied the damned into a cohesive force.

And so he has. Once unified, production quotas Olympos were fulfilled by Pandemonium more reliably -- some like Elias might say, easily. There is no monitoring of Pandemonium -- CHR's eyes have no visibility -- is it possible he has, somehow, broken the hydrogen scarcity problem and is simply biding his time?

### Elias Vane

The grandson of Silas Vane. Elias was a sensitive boy. When he was twelve though he made a fatal mistake. A prisoner bound for transport to Pandemonium saw his face, and, recognizing that this was in fact at least a relative of the great Silas, ran to him, begging for mercy. Silas, distressed, ran to his grandfather, explaining in his childish way, that the man did not wish to go to Pandemonium -- Silas laughed, explained bluntly that obviously no one wanted to go to Pandemonium, and said that he dismissed Elias as a worthy successor, being guilty of the sin of empathy.

Since then Elias has striven to prove he is a person no less merciless than his grandfather, rather quite against his own nature. Each effort has only served to disappoint his grandfather further. At each "effort" he betrays himself as either lacking subtlety in his cruelty -- Silas dismisses these efforts as "performative cruelty" meant rather to shock than to impress -- or too gentle, in which case Silas calls him a weakling.

Elias thinks though he has something at last: inasmuch as he has detected a "hydrogen deficit" being fed to Pandemonium. If Pandemonium were to stockpile hydrogen, the threat of scarcity, and so CHR would be threatened. Elias means to find out the sources of this glut.

### Dr. Colette Renard

Born and raised on Gaia under a Union-member nation, Colette is a soft spoken and gentle climatologist advising the Union on prospects and conditions for terraforming Acheron. If we had to analyze her career,  it was that she was underestimated. She owes her success as much to surprise as to raw but very quiet competence.

She had secretly been an admirer of Thomas Thorne during his revolutionary days. Once he was captured and condemned, she mourned, quietly. Since she learnt he was "under" her she sends him presents now and then as she can, without the notice of her staff, masking it under the guise of operational losses inevitable from operating constantly in clouds of sulfuric acid. A plausible excuse, we must grant her.

Since then Colette has quietly acquired and "bulked" down vaporizers to recover water from bodies and other detritus, more efficient smelters, anything to help the citizens of Pandemonium. And it just so happens that under the spraying rain of sulfuric acid, a fair few of the hydrogen tanks on Nimbus Crucible rupture.
